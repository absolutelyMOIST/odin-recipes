Flautas

Description

Certainly! Flautas are a delicious Mexican dish that involves tortillas rolled tightly around a flavorful filling, typically made with shredded meat, such as chicken or beef. The rolled tortillas are then deep-fried until they become crispy and golden brown. The word "flauta" translates to "flute" in Spanish, and the dish earned its name due to its flute-like appearance. Flautas are often served with toppings like guacamole, sour cream, and salsa, adding a delightful combination of textures and flavors to the overall experience. This culinary creation is not only tasty but also a wonderful representation of the rich and diverse Mexican cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 Onion
  • 1 Jalapeno
  • 1 whole Rotisserie Chicken (shredded)
  • 3 Green Chiles
  • 1 cup Shredded Cheese (Mexican blend)
  • Fresh lime juices
  • Seasonings (ground cumin, chili powder, salt, garlic powder, and paprika)

Steps

  1. First, cook the onion and jalapeño in a little bit of olive oil until softened.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked onion, jalapeño, chicken, seasonings, green chiles, and lime juice. Stir in the shredded cheese.
  3. Use the freshest tortillas you can find, so they don't tear. I also recommend heating them up in the microwave for about 30 seconds so they are pliable.
  4. Place a flour tortilla on a flat surface. Add about 3 tablespoons of the filling to the bottom third of each tortilla then tightly roll up tortillas and place seam side down on the wire rack. Continue rolling until you are out of filling.
  5. Heat canola or vegetable oil in a large skillet and use tongs to place the flautas one by one in the hot oil. Set them on a paper towel-lined plate after cooking!
